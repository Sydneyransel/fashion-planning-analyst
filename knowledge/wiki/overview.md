# SKIMS Brand Overview

*Sources: skims.com/pages/about, wwd.com ($5B valuation), wwd.com (retail expansion), forbes.com*

## What SKIMS Is

SKIMS is a solutions-oriented apparel brand founded in 2018 by Kim Kardashian and co-founder/CEO Jens Grede. It launched as a shapewear brand with a direct-to-consumer model and has since expanded into underwear, bras, loungewear, swim, dresses, and menswear. As of 2024, SKIMS is valued at **$5 billion** following a $225 million capital raise from Goldman Sachs Alternatives and BDT & MSD Partners.

## Founding Philosophy

SKIMS positions itself as a "solutions-oriented brand creating the next generation of underwear, loungewear and shapewear." Its core promise is inclusivity: products designed for every body type, with extended size ranges and fabrics engineered to stretch and adapt. The brand's own language frames it not as fashion but as functional innovation — "setting new standards," "consistently innovating on the past."

## Scale and Financials

- **Valuation:** $5 billion (2024 funding round)
- **Net sales:** Expected to top $1 billion in the funding year
- **Stores:** 18 owned U.S. locations + 2 franchise doors in Mexico
- **Strategic direction:** Targeting a "predominantly physical business over the next few years" — shifting from DTC-first to omnichannel
- **Investors:** Goldman Sachs Alternatives, BDT & MSD Partners, Wellington Management (earlier round)

## Leadership

- **Kim Kardashian** — Founder and face of the brand; 354M Instagram followers; public statements frame SKIMS as a global omnichannel retail brand
- **Jens Grede** — Co-founder and CEO; described growth as "a pivotal next step" in the brand's six-year trajectory

## Strategic Priorities (2024–2026)

1. **Physical retail expansion** — accelerating store openings in U.S. and internationally
2. **International growth** — franchise and owned doors outside U.S.
3. **Category expansion** — NikeSkims collaboration (athleisure), beauty category (hired Diarrha N'Diaye as EVP beauty and fragrance)
4. **Brand awareness amplification** — provocative product drops (e.g., "bush thong") that generate earned media alongside traditional press

## Brand Positioning vs. Competitors

SKIMS competes with Spanx (shapewear incumbents), ThirdLove (bras), Savage X Fenty (inclusive intimates), and now Nike (athleisure via collaboration). Its differentiation is the combination of celebrity founder credibility, fabric innovation (Fits Everybody technology), and size inclusivity marketed as a brand value rather than a feature.

## Key Partnerships and Collaborations

- **NikeSkims** — Major athleisure collaboration launched 2025; generated significant press and search interest
- **FUNBOY** — Pool float collection (2026), extending brand into seasonal lifestyle accessories
- **NBA** — Official underwear and loungewear partner (2023), a landmark deal that expanded male demographic awareness

## Synthesis Note

Across all 15 sources, the consistent narrative is that SKIMS grew from a single category (shapewear) into a multi-category brand by staying true to one positioning: products that solve a problem for a wide range of bodies. Press coverage uniformly credits the NikeSkims collaboration and physical retail push as the two biggest near-term growth levers.
