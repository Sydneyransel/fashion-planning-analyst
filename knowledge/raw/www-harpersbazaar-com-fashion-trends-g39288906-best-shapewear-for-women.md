[Shapewear](https://www.harpersbazaar.com/fashion/trends/g37991040/best-shapewear-for-plus-size/) is an essential component of each and every wardrobe. These pieces build the foundation of any outfit, and are designed to make you look and feel great. It’s not always easy to find the most comfortable shapewear, but once you do, it pays off.

Whether it’s a [slipdress](https://www.harpersbazaar.com/fashion/trends/g43267124/best-slip-dresses/) or [compression shorts](https://www.harpersbazaar.com/fashion/trends/g39194199/best-biker-shorts-for-women/), shapewear comes in all sorts of silhouettes. Caroline Maguire, fashion director at [Shopbop](https://click.linksynergy.com/deeplink?id=SyrN4SGJMyU&mid=42352&u1=har|xid:{xid}&murl=https%3A%2F%2Fwww.shopbop.com%2F), has some advice for picking out the best pieces for you. “Look for styles that offer the support you’re looking for without compromising comfort,” she says. “I always like to go for tagless styles for ultimate comfort. Finding shapewear pieces with quality, durable fabric is also very important.” Keeping all that in mind, we searched high and low for the most comfortable and durable styles. And from Spanx to Skims to Wolford to Commando, we found shaping and sculpting styles from all our favorite brands.

Below, shop our edit of the 15 best shapewear pieces to accentuate your figure while making you feel your very best.

## What to look for when choosing shapewear?

![shape, rectangle](https://hips.hearstapps.com/hmg-prod/images/thick-linebreak-blk-1649967966-6627fd761a347.jpeg?crop=1xw:1xh;center,top)

When it comes to shapewear, you will want to consider how you plan to use it first and foremost. Are you shopping for a specific dress, which would require a lower neckline or fuller thigh coverage? Or are you looking for a piece you could wear every day, even on its own, like a long-sleeved bodysuit? How you intend to wear the piece will determine the cut you want. Then you want to look for a material that is soft and stretchy, like a modal or spandex blend. Finally, prioritize comfort. Shapewear might be designed to hold you in, but that doesn’t mean it has to do so uncomfortably. Read reviews, try different pieces, and find the style that’s best for you.
