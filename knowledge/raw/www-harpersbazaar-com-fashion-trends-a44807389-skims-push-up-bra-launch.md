The heyday of the push-up bra came and went ages before [Kim Kardashian](https://www.harpersbazaar.com/beauty/health/a43263235/kim-kardashian-self-care-favorite-products-interview/) launched shapewear label [Skims](https://skims.wsktbf.net/c/3006986/618913/10056?u=https%3A%2F%2Fskims.com%2Fcollections%2Fcoming-soon&subId1=har&subid3=xid:{xid}) in 2019. Women [are buying](https://www.globenewswire.com/en/news-release/2023/04/18/2649178/0/en/Global-Lingerie-Market-is-anticipated-to-grow-further-at-6-48-during-2023-2028-Bonafide-Research.html) unpadded styles more than any other on the lingerie market. Personally, I can’t even remember the last time I willingly wore a bra stuffed with extra.

Kardashian can guess why. “I think women stopped wearing push-up bras because they were made without considering comfort and were designed to only do one thing: push your boobs up,” she tells _Bazaar_. “They weren’t practical and didn’t create a natural-looking shape that would then show spillage under clothing.” She has had her own less-than-enjoyable experiences with the style: “Traditional push-up bras would give me a lift, but didn’t create a natural shape, and gave me a shelflike look from the side, distorting the curves of my bust.”

Skims’ next major release attempts to do what few push-up bras can: support and lift in a way that women will actually want to wear. On Thursday, August 17, the label will release its so-called [“Ultimate Bra,”](https://skims.wsktbf.net/c/3006986/618913/10056?u=https%3A%2F%2Fskims.com%2Fcollections%2Fcoming-soon&subId1=har&subid3=xid:{xid}) a style Kardashian describes as “totally different” from the bulky, shelflike designs of the early 2000s. Created with 3-D scans of several women—including Kardashian—the bra’s cups are modeled with a lightweight, tapered foam pad for graded fullness and a more natural look. It’s the latest in a long list of comfort-driven items winning over shapewear skeptics and taking Skims to a [$4 billion valuation](https://www.businessoffashion.com/news/retail/kim-kardashians-skims-reaches-4-billion-valuation/#:~:text=Skims%2C%20the%20shapewear%20and%20intimates,billion%2C%20the%20company%20announced%20Wednesday.), including its asymmetric compressive shorts, sexy yet supportive [swimwear](https://www.harpersbazaar.com/fashion/trends/a40705691/skims-swim-review/), and a shaping bodysuit with fabric so strong, one woman claims it [saved her from a bullet](https://www.tiktok.com/@honeygxd/video/7234610033911172398).

![a model wears the new skims push up bra](https://hips.hearstapps.com/hmg-prod/images/10-emily-ultimatebra-bronze-0373-x3a-final-64da4a609f4e9.jpg?resize=980:*)

Courtesy SKIMS

The Ultimate Bra will retail for $58, in 34 sizes and 10 colors. It’s made from a compressive microfiber fabric that’s both soft and flexible; in the cups, tapered foam creates a teardrop shape that lifts but doesn’t squeeze.

All of those 3-D scans informed how the foam could be precisely distributed to nail the shape, Kardashian says. Then,“we also went through multiple rounds of fittings on myself and other women to perfect the shape and construction even further.”

Skims co-founder and CEO Jens Grede referred to push-up bras as a category “ripe for disruption.” The brand released a version of the style with a soft underwire last year; the Ultimate Bra is the next evolution. “People have always said the first thing they take off when they get home is their underwire bra,” Kardashian explains, “so when we launched the category last year, that was the main thing we wanted to change.”

![a model wears a skims push up bra and skims thong](https://hips.hearstapps.com/hmg-prod/images/23-miki-ultimatebra-onyx-0693-x3a-final-64da4a9e9ccba.jpg?resize=980:*)

Courtesy SKIMS

For her part, Kardashian plans to wear the new Ultimate style for casual, everyday moments when she’d have previously avoided a push-up bra. “I love how the bra naturally enhances my shape underneath our cotton jersey T-Shirt,” she says. “So I’d wear it with the bra styled back to jeans or pants for a weekend look, or for family dinners at home.”
